A timeline of my work and achievements:

  • Interested in game development, I started my graphic design work at the age of 10.
  • I started off just out of passion for video games, which would soon turn into a passion for computers. I would use simple drag and drop engines like Game Maker: Studio to build simple games, and would make the sprites and animations myself. That is where I first got interested in game and graphic design.
  • Being curious and wanting to have more control over what I did, at the age of 13 I started learning Java.
  • I had sharpened my Graphic Design skills by then. Java was my first programming language, and I started learning it out of sheer curiosity. What I didn't know was that it would spark the love for programming and computers in me.
  • On the age of 14, I joined Frozen Vortex Games and participated in the development of Life Simulator, which accumulated over 2 million downloads.
  • This was probably one of the most crucial points in my life, as this success helped me define what I wanted to do in the future. The game is still accumulating more and more fans everyday.
  • When I was about to turn 16, I left Frozen Vortex Games to pursue web development.
  • Probably a really bold move, but I had already started learning more and more about web development, and it had really started sparking interest in me. I can now proudly say I am an expert at it, and it is what I hope to do for a living in the future.
  • I am now working on back-end development, among other technologies that captivate me
  • I am confident in my skills, but also confident I can sharpen them even further. I hope to expand my portfolio even more everyday. I really like the way my life has been going, and I couldn't have asked for more.
